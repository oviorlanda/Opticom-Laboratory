The Village Development Program (Bina Desa) is an initiative by Opticom Laboratory aimed at improving the quality of life in rural areas through the application of telecommunications technology and the empowerment of local communities. This program reflects our commitment to social responsibility and the application of academic knowledge for community welfare.

"Bridging the digital divide in rural areas through telecommunications infrastructure and digital literacy training."

Through collaboration with local governments, non-governmental organizations, and village communities, the Bina Desa program focuses on bridging the digital divide in rural areas. We introduce basic telecommunications infrastructure, provide training on digital literacy, and demonstrate how optical communication technology can improve access to information and services in remote villages.

Our students and laboratory assistants actively participate in this program, conducting field surveys, installing basic communication equipment, and providing workshops on the use of digital technology for various applications including education, healthcare access, and local economic development.

The Open Laboratory program is a flagship initiative of Opticom Laboratory designed to promote knowledge sharing, research collaboration, and public engagement with optical communication technology. This program opens our laboratory facilities to students, researchers, industry professionals, and the general public, providing access to state-of-the-art equipment and expertise in the field of optical communications.

"Fostering a vibrant ecosystem of learning and innovation through hands-on experience with cutting-edge optical communication technology."

During Open Laboratory sessions, we conduct demonstrations of various optical communication systems, showcase ongoing research projects, and provide hands-on experiences with fiber optic equipment. Participants have the opportunity to observe practical experiments, learn about cutting-edge technologies such as Visible Light Communication and Free Space Optics, and interact with our research team and laboratory assistants.

The program also serves as a platform for organizing study groups, technical workshops, and collaborative research sessions. We invite industry partners, alumni, and researchers from other institutions to share their experiences and insights, fostering a vibrant ecosystem of learning and innovation.
